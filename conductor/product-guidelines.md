# Product Guidelines: School Bookclub Manager

## 1. Tone and Voice
- **Student-Focused:** The language should be warm and supportive, making students feel encouraged in their reading journey.
- **Simplicity & Clarity:** Avoid overly academic or complex jargon. Use simple, direct instructions that are easy for students of various ages to understand.
- **Clean & Focused:** Keep communication focused on the task at hand (reading and logging) to minimize distraction.

## 2. Visual Style
- **Modern & Clean:** The interface should feel contemporary and professional yet approachable.
- **Clarity over Clutter:** Prioritize whitespace and clear hierarchy to help users focus on core actions.
- **Consistent Branding:** Use a cohesive color palette and typography that feels unified across the entire platform.

## 3. UX Principles
- **Effortless Entry:** Logging a book or a reading session should require as few steps as possible. Use smart defaults and automated lookups where applicable.
- **Self-Explanatory:** The UI should guide the user. Clear labels, tooltips, and helpful "empty states" should ensure that first-time users know exactly what to do.
- **Accessibility-First:** Design for inclusivity. Ensure high contrast, readable font sizes, and compatibility with assistive technologies to support students with varying needs.

## 4. Interaction and Feedback
- **Subtle Confirmation:** Provide clear but understated feedback when actions are successful (e.g., a simple checkmark or a brief success toast).
- **Responsive Interaction:** Ensure the UI feels "alive" with immediate feedback for user inputs, without being overwhelming or distracting.
- **Consistency:** Use the same patterns for similar actions throughout the app to build user confidence and familiarity.
